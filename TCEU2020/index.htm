TCEU 2020 - Amsterdam

Link to map: :lick here:

Amsterdam is our capitol so anything you want should be feasible. Although some tastes might require a metro ride (there is a station underneath the venue).
If youre also in Amsterdam on noncon days, Id recommend taking the metro into the city center to explore the city!

There are a bunch of eating possibilities from RAI itself (or partnered) near or within the venue. These are listed here.
https://www.rai.nl/en/visiting/foodanddrinks/foodanddrinksintertraffic/

For now, Ill focus on whats near the venue and within walking distance. (During my walk it rained a lot, so most terraces are closed off or look messy due to staff removing pillows etc.)

Directly beneath the venue there is a small park where you can escape the crowds; it has a bunch of stops/gyms in PoGo too.


There should be a restaurant in the park ( Resteaurant As), but the building wasnt looking all that inviting when I checked (fenced off). So, it might have closed or something.

There is also a small drinks/bites place at the waterside when you walk there StrandZuid. They are closed for the season now and actually open up for the first time on May 1st!
As this venue is located on the RAI grounds expect it to be crowded!

This food truck with French fries is partnered with RAI, and always to be found somewhere on the venue grounds:



Right next to the venue (no need to cross the S109) youll find THE ROAST ROOM Which is a nice big place, but so close to RAI that I expect it to be busy, I do think they are best equipped to handle groups, but you wont be the only group from TCEU in there for sure!

If you cross the S109 near the above restaurant youll find a variety of places to eat, sit drink or snack.

Visaandeschelde (fish)
The Corner
Chutimas (thai food - no website)
De Overkant

The street ('Scheldestraat) between De Overkant and The Corner is filled with more eating establishements. Due to the weather I had to cut my walk short, so I didnt check these out :

During my walk I took the liberty of testing one of the venues near here called De nieuwe Rai a cozy place with proper indoor seating and toilets.
They advertise as burgerbar but also make custom sandwiches etc. If its not to crowded this might be a nice place to sit with a group.



Personally, this would be my choice with a group if the roast room was too crowded or not picked for other reasons.

Nanbu (sushi)
Colosseo < DO NOT GO THERE - Awfull food!!


Knabbel en babbel (diner/café)
Toetanchamon (grillroom/greek) < This place has mixed reviews online and the building/interior didnt look that appealing to me.

If you feel like a 'fancy dinner, there are a bunch of new hotels built right next to the venue (near the train station) with fine dining options. And if you dont mind queuing for a quick pizza happy Italy might be your thing.
For a group though, Id stick with venues with at least toilets, some seating and a nice menu that includes vegan options.

In this area there is also a shopping mall with a supermarket, drugstore and 'Action (which is basically an AliExpress store, useful if you forgot to pack a charger/cable/earbuds. Or if you just need a cheap BT speaker).
There is also 2 food establishments in there if you fancy this type of food (see pictures).

Kreeftenbar - (means lobsterbar) seems to also sell sushi

Or lunchroom - Vandaag

TL;DR: imho with a medium sized group I would opt for: THE ROAST ROOM first, with the option to divert to something smaller like De nieuwe Rai as an alternative.
